Prompt
It is the input or instruction given to an AI model to generate a response.

Prompt Engg
it is the process of crafting and refining prompts to improve the peerformance of ai models.

Key Parts of Prompt
1. Directive
It is a concise instruction to AI model to perform a task.
2. Examples
3. Role (persona)
4. output formatting
5. additional information (context/knowledge base)

There is no order in above parts.
But what order matters is like:
    1. <context+examples+knowledge base>
    2. <directive>
    3. <output format>
This matters as the flow of information processing matters.

Techniques
1. Instructions in promptss
2. Roles in Prompt
    - a technique that involves assigning a role or persona to an AI model
3. Zero/One-shot to Few-shot (examples in prompt)
    - Also called In-Context Learning
    - shots refer to the no of examples included in the prompt

In-context learning vs fine-tuning a model
- context learning and fine-tuning solve different problems.

    - In-context learning means you give the model examples or instructions in the prompt at inference time. 
        The model uses them immediately without changing its weights.
    - Fine-tuning means you train the model on a dataset so its weights are updated and it behaves differently more permanently.

- One more distinction:
    > Few-shot is better for flexibility and quick iteration.
    > Fine-tuning is better for specialization and scale.

Prompt Priming
Priming chatbots (also known as prompt priming or inception prompts1) is a powerful technique used to guide a chatbot's behavior in a desired direction. 
By framing or structuring the initial prompt, you can influence how the AI interprets user input and tailor its responses to fit specific objectives.

Intermediate section:
A prompting technique may incorporate conditional or branching logic, parallelism, or other architectural considerations spanning multiple prompt.

chain-of-thought(cot) prompting
> a technique that enhances the reasoning capabilities of large language models (LLMs) by incorporating logical steps—or a “chain of thought”—within the prompt 
> encourages multi step reasoning,achieves efficiency w/o finetuning
> according to CoT authors1, CoT only yields performance gains when used with models of ∼100B parameters.

zero-shot chain-of-thought prompting
> extension of cot
> just append at last: lets think step by step

Self-consistency
> It is an approach that simply asks a model the same prompt multiple times and takes the majority result as the final answer.
> follow up to cot prompting
> Wang et al. discuss a more complex method for selecting the final answer, which deals with the Large Language Model's (LLM) generated probabilities for each Chain-of-Thought.

Generated Knowledge
> ask llm to generate potentially useful info about a given promt before generating a final response.

least-to-most prompting
Definition: Least-to-Most (LtM) prompting breaks down problems into simpler subproblems and solves them sequentially.
Comparison with Chain-of-Thought: Unlike Chain-of-Thought prompting, where each step is independent, LtM uses the output of previous subproblems as input for the next.

meta prompting
> asking ai to give you a prompt

dealing with long-form content (as models have limited context length)
1. preprocessing the text (removing unncessary text)
2. chunking and iterative approach
3. code libraries
    - llama index (perform vector search to find which part is relevant) 
    - langchain (perform recursive summaries)

basic llm settings
1. temperature
    > regulates the unpredictability of a language model's output.
    > higher temp: more creative
    > low temp: more predictable

2. top-p
    > helps manage the randomness of their output
    > establishing a probability threshold and then selecting tokens whose combined likelihood surpasses this limit.

3. maximum length
    > total # of tokens the AI is allowed to generate

4. stop sequences
    > tell the output model when to cease output generation.
    like if stop seq: thankyou, so llm will generate uptil thankyou

5. frequency penalty
    > discourages repetition in the generated text

6. presence penalty
    > penalizes tokens based on if they have occured or not, instead of propotionality

NOTE: even when temp and top-p are set to zero,AI may not give same exact output every time.
this is due to randomness in GPU calculations (floating point errors)

Advanced section:
1. Zero-shot prompting
    a) Emotion Prompting1: 
        Leverages emotional language to improve LLM accuracy and response quality by tapping into emotion-rich training data.
    b) Re-reading (RE2)2: 
        Enhances reasoning by asking the model to re-read the prompt, ensuring important details aren't missed.
    c) Rephrase and Respond (RaR)3: 
        Asks the model to rephrase the prompt before answering, reducing ambiguity and improving clarity.
    d) Role Prompting4: 
        Assigns roles to the LLM, creating more context-specific, relevant responses for various tasks.
    e) System 2 Attention (S2A)5: 
        Filters out irrelevant information by having the model refine the prompt, leading to more accurate outputs.
    f) SimToM6: 
        enhances LLMs' ability to understand and predict human thoughts and actions.
