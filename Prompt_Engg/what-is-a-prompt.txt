Prompt
It is the input or instruction given to an AI model to generate a response.

Prompt Engg
it is the process of crafting and refining prompts to improve the peerformance of ai models.

Key Parts of Prompt
1. Directive
It is a concise instruction to AI model to perform a task.
2. Examples
3. Role (persona)
4. output formatting
5. additional information (context/knowledge base)

There is no order in above parts.
But what order matters is like:
    1. <context+examples+knowledge base>
    2. <directive>
    3. <output format>
This matters as the flow of information processing matters.

Techniques
1. Instructions in promptss
2. Roles in Prompt
    - a technique that involves assigning a role or persona to an AI model
3. Zero/One-shot to Few-shot (examples in prompt)
    - Also called In-Context Learning
    - shots refer to the no of examples included in the prompt

In-context learning vs fine-tuning a model
- context learning and fine-tuning solve different problems.

    - In-context learning means you give the model examples or instructions in the prompt at inference time. 
        The model uses them immediately without changing its weights.
    - Fine-tuning means you train the model on a dataset so its weights are updated and it behaves differently more permanently.

- One more distinction:
    > Few-shot is better for flexibility and quick iteration.
    > Fine-tuning is better for specialization and scale.

Prompt Priming
Priming chatbots (also known as prompt priming or inception prompts1) is a powerful technique used to guide a chatbot's behavior in a desired direction. 
By framing or structuring the initial prompt, you can influence how the AI interprets user input and tailor its responses to fit specific objectives.

Chatbots vs Large language models(LLMs)
